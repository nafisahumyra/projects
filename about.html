About Me

I am a dedicated and results-driven IT professional with a robust academic background in Information Technology and a double minor in Computer Science and Psychology. My passion for cybersecurity has been honed through hands-on experience as an IT support technician and a cybersecurity intern, where I gained practical knowledge in network security, vulnerability assessments, and system administration.

During my academic journey, I collaborated on research focused on covert channels, providing me with a deeper understanding of advanced security concepts and threats. This experience has equipped me with the analytical skills and technical expertise necessary to identify and mitigate security vulnerabilities effectively.

I am particularly enthusiastic about applying my skills to support the mission of the NSA. My commitment to continuous learning and professional growth drives me to stay updated on the latest developments and trends in cybersecurity. I thrive in collaborative environments, working closely with stakeholders to develop and implement robust security solutions. Because in cybersecurity, as in life, it’s all about having the right people on your team—and a healthy paranoia about unsolicited emails.

In my career, I aim to leverage my technical acumen, problem-solving abilities, and passion for cybersecurity to protect critical information systems and contribute to national security efforts. The best way to catch a hacker is to think like one, and preferably, think faster.
